03.12.2016

I’m currently chaperoning a group of 12 students on a trip to Amsterdam, Brussels, and Paris. Each day of the trip, someone will post a blog about their experience. Today is my turn.

To the friends and family following our journey on social media:
While the photographs we have posted undoubtedly tell a beautiful and exciting story, the most real and incredible moments in traveling are the ones we can’t take pictures of. Let me explain.

There are two groups of students on this tour—one from the great state of Texas, and the other—mighty Mississippi. Our tour director is a native Australian and German resident, and I hail from sweet home Alabama. Last night as we were driving through the Belgian countryside, I made my way to the front of the bus to serve my duty as DJ. I buckled into the jumpseat next to our Dutch bus driver, Thomas, and started the party with a timeless classic by Justin Bieber.
My view from the passenger seat revealed flat farm land extending for miles, and the stars were bright and innumerable. Justin Bieber’s voice continued to serenade the bus full of students as I searched my iPod for the next song. I pressed play and the Beatles’ “Hey Jude” sounded through the speakers. A few verses into the song, I realized everyone was singing: the Texans, the Mississippi folk, the Aussie, and even the Dutchman bus-driver. At that moment, the differences in culture, accent, and appearance suddenly faded away. We were just people who liked music, driving through Europe on a large, Pepto Bismol colored bus—together.

Instagram, Facebook, and SnapChat can’t tell you this. Pictures are worth a thousand words, and words can concretely present thoughts and feelings, but there is no substitute for the actual experience. No set of vernacular can accurately explain the deeper understanding of culture and people that you gain when you travel. Experiences can change you, and I’m confident that the ones we’ve shared on this trip will play a part in shaping each one of us for the better.

Pictures will fade, but the beauty of authentic experience will not. That moment of musical togetherness on the Pepto Bismol bus was an awakening one. It was a real and incredible moment, and I couldn’t take a picture of it, nor would I want to.
